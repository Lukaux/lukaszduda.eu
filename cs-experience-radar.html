Experience radar

AI-powered user experience monitoring

Context

Within the project team, we wanted to deepen our understanding of users' problems and needs. We needed a research tool that would allow us to continuously monitor user opinions about the service and support both business and product decisions. In response, I initiated a project of periodic quantitative and qualitative research, supported by AI.

Key achievements

  • I detected dozens of changes in users experience.
  • I identified key user problems and unmet needs.
  • I discovered 100+ improvement ideas and turned them into design tasks.
  • I discovered 50+ technical issues and turned them into developer-ready tasks.
  • I introduced 58 valuable UX metrics tied to real user feedback.

Process

Data collection

The first step was to collect as much user feedback as possible. I identified and connected multiple sources, including customer support tickets, surveys, and app store reviews. To increase the diversity of insights, I introduced additional survey-based research. I worked closely with customer support and development teams to ensure a continuous and reliable data flow. As a result, the system now processes approximately 20,000 user opinions per month.

Defining research categories

The collected data had to be structured into research categories. I used the Umami method and card sorting to develop a clear metadata structure. I selected 51 topics users talk about (e.g. login, reading the interface, communication). These topics were grouped into 6 types of feedback, such as issues, praise, or lack of information on a given topic.

Data security

Before using AI to categorize user feedback, I ensured user data was properly protected. In collaboration with the backend team, we created a simple anonymization tool. The program removes names, surnames, email addresses, and phone numbers from the data.

AI automation

The volume of user feedback made manual analysis impossible. Together with developers, we used Make to connect Google Sheets with OpenAI. When prepared user comments are uploaded to the table, the tool automatically assigns research categories. After several experiments, I developed a prompt that reliably classifies feedback into the correct categories.

Reporting

I publish the analyzed data in a monthly report. The report shows which problems and needs users have, broken down by service version (Web, Android, iOS). It highlights the ratio of identified issues relative to MAU, presents trends from the last six months, and describes the most important insights.

Creating tasks for the team

An additional value of working with user feedback is identifying small improvement tasks within the service. I extract comments that indicate potential interface issues. These topics are regularly discussed with the product team, and together we create tasks to address them. As a result, we delivered over a hundred quick-win tasks to the backlog.
